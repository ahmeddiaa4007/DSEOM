The Rise of Influencer Marketing: Why It Works and How to Do It Right?

Influencer marketing is a type of marketing that uses social media influencers to promote a product or service. Influencers are people who have a large following on social media and who are considered to be experts in a particular field.

When an influencer promotes a product or service, their followers are more likely to trust the recommendation and buy the product.

Influencer marketing has become increasingly popular in recent years. In 2019, the global influencer marketing market was worth $9.7 billion and is expected to reach $23.3 billion by 2023.

There are a number of reasons for the rise of influencer marketing, including:

  • The Rise of Social Media: Social media has made it easier for people to connect with each other and share information. This has created a large audience for influencers, who can reach a wider range of people than traditional advertising methods.

  • The Rise of Trust in Influencers: People are more likely to trust recommendations from people they know and trust. Influencers have built relationships with their followers, which makes their recommendations more credible.

  • The Effectiveness of Influencer Marketing: Influencer marketing has been shown to be effective in generating leads, sales, and brand awareness. A study by Nielsen found that 66% of consumers trust recommendations from influencers.

Why It Works?


Influencer marketing works because it taps into the power of social proof. Social proof is the idea that people are more likely to do something if they see that other people are doing it.

When an influencer promotes a product or service, their followers are more likely to trust the recommendation and buy the product.

It is also effective because it is relevant to the target audience. Influencers typically have a large following of people who are interested in the same things they are.

This makes it more likely that the influencer's audience will be interested in the product or service being promoted.

“People do not buy goods and services. They buy relations, stories, and magic.”
By Seth Godin

How to Do It Right?


There are a few things to keep in mind when doing influencer marketing:

  • Choose the Right Influencers: Not all influencers are created equal. It is important to choose influencers who have a large following of people who are interested in the product or service being promoted.

  • Set Clear Goals: What do you want to achieve with your influencer marketing campaign? Do you want to generate leads, sales, or brand awareness?

  • Create a Creative Brief: A creative brief will help you to communicate your goals to the influencers you work with. It should include information about your product or service, your target audience, and your desired outcome.

  • Track Your Results: It is important to track the results of your influencer marketing campaign so that you can see what is working and what is not. You can use tools like Google Analytics to track website traffic, social media engagement, and sales.


Here are some additional tips for doing influencer marketing right:


  • Be Authentic: Influencers are more likely to promote products or services that they genuinely believe in. If you try to force an influencer to promote something they don't care about, it will be obvious and your campaign will likely fail.

  • Be Transparent: Be clear about the fact that you are working with an influencer and that they are being compensated for their promotion. This will help to build trust with your audience and avoid any potential backlash.

  • Be Creative: Don't just ask influencers to post a photo of your product with a generic caption. Get creative and come up with ideas for campaigns that will engage your audience and make them more likely to take action.

Conclusion


Influencer marketing is a powerful marketing tool that can be used to reach a large audience and generate leads, sales, and brand awareness.

By following the tips above, you can do influencer marketing right and achieve your marketing goals.
