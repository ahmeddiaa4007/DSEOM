SEO Glossary & Definitions


Book Your Consultation
Term Definition
SEO (Search Engine Optimization) The practice of optimizing a website to improve its visibility and rankings on search engine results pages (SERPs).
SERP (Search Engine Results Page) The page displayed by search engines in response to a user's query, listing relevant websites and other content.
Keyword A word or phrase that represents the main topic or theme of a webpage, used to optimize content for targeted searches.
Backlink A link from one website to another, which helps establish authority and improves organic search rankings.
Organic Traffic Natural, non-paid search engine results driven by relevance and quality rather than advertising.
On-Page SEO Optimization techniques applied directly on webpages such as keyword optimization, meta tags, headings, etc., to improve visibility in SERPs.
Off-Page SEO Techniques implemented outside the website itself to improve its ranking signals; includes backlink building and social media marketing.
Meta Tags HTML tags providing information about a webpage's content for search engines; includes title tags and meta descriptions.
Title Tag An HTML element specifying the title of a webpage that appears as the clickable headline in SERPs.
Meta Description Tag An HTML element providing concise summaries describing the content of webpages appearing in SERPs below the title tag.
Alt Text/Attribute Descriptive text added to an image tag specifying alternative text when images cannot be displayed; useful for accessibility and SEO purposes.
Page Speed The time it takes for a webpage to load completely; faster loading times are favored by both users and search engines.
Crawlability Refers to how easily search engine bots can navigate through your site’s pages
Indexability Determines whether your site’s pages can be indexed by Google
XML Sitemap A file listing all the pages on a website, helping search engines understand its structure and index content more effectively.
Robots.txt A text file that tells search engine bots which pages or sections of a website should not be crawled or indexed.
Domain Authority (DA) A score developed by Moz indicating the overall authority and quality of a website; higher scores correlate with better rankings.
Page Authority (PA) Similar to DA, but evaluates individual webpage authority based on factors such as backlinks and content relevance.
Anchor Text The clickable text in a hyperlink; optimizing anchor text can improve keyword visibility and relevancy for linked pages.
Keyword Density The percentage of times a targeted keyword appears compared to the total word count on a webpage; excessive density may be penalized as spammy.
Long-Tail Keywords Longer, more specific keywords often used when users are closer to making purchasing decisions; tend to have lower competition than broader terms.
Local SEO Optimizing online presence for local searches by targeting specific geographical locations
Google My Business Google's platform allowing businesses to create profiles that appear in local search results
CTA (Click-Through-Rate) The percentage of people who click on an ad or organic search result after seeing it displayed
Bounce Rate Percentage of visitors who leave your site without interacting further after viewing only one page. High bounce rates can indicate poor user experience or irrelevant content.
Conversion Rate The percentage of website visitors who complete a desired action, such as making a purchase or filling out a form.
Landing Page A specific webpage designed to attract and convert users, often created for targeted marketing campaigns.
Canonical URL The preferred version of a webpage that search engines should index when there are multiple versions with similar content.
Mobile Optimization Ensuring that websites are optimized for mobile devices in terms of design, usability, and load times.
Responsive Design Creating websites that automatically adjust their layout and content based on the user's device screen size.
Schema Markup Structured data markup added to webpages to help search engines understand the context and meaning of content.
Site Architecture The organization and hierarchical structure of pages within a website; impacts crawlability and user experience.
Organic Traffic Visitors who land on a website through unpaid (organic) search engine results rather than paid advertisements or direct links.
Nofollow Link/Attribute An HTML attribute used by webmasters to instruct search engines not to follow or pass authority through certain outbound links.
Rich Snippets Additional information displayed beneath SERP listings providing more context about the page’s content
Local Pack Localized organic search results displaying businesses relevant to a particular location
Keyword Research Process of identifying popular keywords related to your business niche for targeting in SEO strategies
Social Signals Metrics indicating social media engagement with your website’s content; can impact rankings indirectly.
Content Marketing Developing valuable, informative, and engaging content with the goal of attracting and retaining an audience.
Inbound Marketing Strategy focused on drawing customers towards products/services organically rather than interruptive advertising methods.
Duplicate Content Identical or very similar content appearing on multiple webpages, which can cause indexing issues and potential penalties.
Keyword Stuffing Overusing keywords in an unnatural way within content to manipulate search engine rankings; frowned upon by search engines.
User Experience (UX) The overall experience a user has when interacting with a website, including design, navigation, and ease of use.
Internal Linking Adding hyperlinks within your website's content that direct users to other relevant pages on your site; helps with navigation and SEO.
Information Architecture Organization and structuring of website content for easy navigation
Doorway Page Low-quality page created solely for ranking purposes without providing value to visitors
Pagerank Algorithm developed by Google co-founder Larry Page that determines the importance/authority of webpages based on backlinks.
Domain Name The unique address that identifies a website on the internet (e.g., www.example.com).
SSL Certificate A digital certificate that encrypts data transmitted between a user's browser and a web server, ensuring secure communication.
Canonicalization The process of selecting the preferred URL when there are multiple versions of a webpage with similar content.
Redirect A permanent redirect from one URL to another, often used when moving or renaming webpages.
Error Page The page displayed when a requested URL is not found; it informs users and search engines that the content doesn't exist.
Domain Age The length of time since a domain was first registered; older domains may have more authority in search rankings
Site Audit Comprehensive analysis of website performance, identifying SEO issues and areas for improvement
Penalty Negative impact imposed by search engines on websites violating their guidelines through practices like spammy backlinks or keyword stuffing.
