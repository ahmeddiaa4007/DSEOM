Term Definition
Social Media Marketing The practice of using social media platforms to promote products, services, or brands and engage with target audiences.
Engagement The level of interaction and involvement that users have with content on social media platforms, such as likes, comments, and shares.
Reach The total number of unique users who see a particular piece of content on social media.
Impressions The total number of times a piece of content is displayed on users' screens in their feeds or timelines.
Hashtags A word or phrase preceded by the "#" symbol used to categorize and group related posts together for easier discovery on social media platforms.
Influencer An individual with a significant following and influence on social media who can impact consumer behavior through endorsements
User-Generated Content (UGC) Content created by consumers/users about a brand or product; often shared organically
Viral Content Content that spreads rapidly across the internet through sharing and engagement
Social Listening Monitoring conversations happening online around specific keywords/brand mentions to gather insights
Content Calendar A schedule outlining planned content topics, formats, and publishing dates for effective planning
Brand Awareness The extent to which consumers are familiar with and recognize a particular brand within its target market.
Call-to-Action (CTA) A prompt encouraging users to take a specific action after engaging with content; examples include "Shop Now" or "Sign Up."
Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO) Techniques aimed at increasing the percentage of website visitors who complete desired actions like making purchases or filling out forms.
Demographics Statistical data relating to specific characteristics (age, gender, location) that define an audience segment.
A/B Testing Comparing two versions (A & B) of a webpage or content to determine which performs better based on predefined goals.
Engagement Rate A measure of the level of interaction an audience has with social media content, often expressed as a percentage.
KPI (Key Performance Indicator) Metrics used to evaluate the success of social media campaigns, such as reach, engagement rate, conversions, etc.
Reach vs Impressions Reach refers to unique users who see your content while impressions are total views including multiple views by the same user
Social Media Manager The person responsible for planning, executing, and managing social media marketing strategies for a brand or business.
Organic Reach The number of users who see a piece of content without any paid promotion or advertising.
Paid Advertising Promotional activities on social media platforms that require payment for increased reach and visibility.
Social Media Analytics Tools and data analysis techniques used to track and measure the performance of social media campaigns.
Content Strategy A plan outlining what type of content will be created, how it will be distributed across different channels
Brand Voice The unique personality and style in which a brand communicates its messages on social media
Crisis Management Strategies implemented during times of PR crisis or negative feedback on social media
Influencer Outreach Initiating contact with influencers to build relationships for potential collaborations
Social Proof Positive validation gained through likes, shares, testimonials from customers/users
Employee Advocacy Program Encouraging employees to actively promote your brand’s message/content on their personal networks
Customer Relationship Management (CRM) Managing customer interactions throughout their lifecycle; can involve tracking interactions via social media
Shareability Measure indicating how likely people are to share your content within their networks.
Social Media Campaign A coordinated series of activities and content designed to achieve specific marketing goals on social media platforms.
User Persona A fictional representation of your target audience based on market research and data, used to inform social media strategies.
Trendjacking Leveraging current trends or popular topics in social media conversations to gain visibility and engagement for your brand.
Dark Social Refers to private sharing of content through messaging apps or email that is difficult to track using traditional analytics tools.
Geotagging Adding geographical location metadata (longitude/latitude) to posts/photos/videos
Ad Fatigue When an audience becomes tired or unresponsive due to being repeatedly exposed to the same ad
Retargeting Displaying ads specifically targeted at users who have previously visited your website/app
Sentiment Analysis Using natural language processing techniques to determine public opinion towards a brand/product/service
Community Management Engaging with online communities/followers by responding, moderating discussions, providing support.
Brand Advocates Highly satisfied customers who actively promote your brand out of their own volition.
Content Curation Finding, selecting, and sharing relevant third-party content with your audience as part of your overall strategy.
Affiliate Marketing Collaborating with affiliates who earn commission for driving traffic/sales through unique referral links.
